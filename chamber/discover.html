Discover Salt Lake City

This area is now known for a giant painted whale statue in the middle of a roundabout. It also hosts several wonderful restaurants and dessert spots. The 9 line bike trail makes this a pleasant place to travel on foot, by bike, or even in a car.

Temple Square

Temple Square, located in downtown Salt Lake City and home to the Salt Lake Temple, contains some of the most beautiful flower beds in the entire valley. You may also wish to visit the Tabernacle or learn more about the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints or attend one of the free concerts hosted at Temple Square.

Utah State Fairpark

0"

The Utah State Fairpark hosts all kinds of events, from the state fair to the Days of '47 Rodeo. There is also a world-class skate park on site. It is located along the Jordan River Parkway if you happen to be traveling by pedal power or on foot.

International Peace Gardens

Located at Jordan Park (~ 1000 South on 900 West ), the International Peace Gardens consists of individual country gardens (Japan shown above) and expresses the hope for peace among nations. Various gardens represent different countries of the world through structures, sculpture, and plant species. It was dedicated in 1952. The Sugarhouse Streetcar Line

Connecting the Trax Light Rail line to the Sugarhouse Area, the S-Line travels from the Central Point Station to McClelland Street near Fairmont Park. A walking and biking trail also extends along its length and provides excellent transportation alternatives to the automobile.
